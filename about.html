artist's statement

keely kuester

a-font-of-type.js was born from a beginner's perspective. Barely a month into working at Skeuomorph Press, I threw out an idea, and it's been tugging at me ever since.
I spent most of my time setting and redistributing type- both for usefulness' sake and to better learn the layout of the California case. Picking this up didn't take long, but impatient as I am, I developed a strategy to increase my efficiency. I noticed each individual word had a distinct path. By memorizing the pathway of the letters 't,' 'h,' and 'e' through the case, I was able to redistribute the entire word 'the' from memory- as one sequence of movements- rather than searching around for each individual letter. Pulling back, then, I realized that each word's pattern would be strung together by spacing, also present within the job case. If words could be mapped, so could sentences - so could entire texts.
From a pencil sketch on tracing paper to an interactive program in JavaScript, I've found this visualization striking at every stage of development. What began as an artistic concept has evolved into a fascinating lens through which aspects of typesetting and text continue to surface. These graphs contain the movements of countless individuals, serving to visualize the typographical DNA which composes a text. I experience hand-setting established content as an echo - as I move, so too did Whitman, so too did the individual setting the next edition of Whitman. This experience is made accessible to those not physically involved by the visualization of said movement.
In a way, this project falls under preservation. Human movement through space is ultimately intangible- instantly carried away by time. I'm not certain which atoms I disturbed in my linoleum floor when I made my coffee this morning, and I'll never have a way of knowing.This visualization retrieves this information, serving as a preservation of the lines humans carve through space.
I'm also struck by how this changes my notion of text itself - hand-set texts contain both the journey and the destination. This imaging maps a text's journey through a California case, but the text itself is what contains these points, their ordering. The text serves as the function, the case as the coordinate system. The text is a map of the humans who composed it. a-font-of-type.js merely brings this latent content to the surface.

This project exists because of support from & collaboration with the fantastic Kadin Henningsen.
